Although we understood the need to take a Fourier transform of the image (a shown in [this](http://phelpsforpresident.github.io/MIC-XRD-Polymer/2014/09/17/2-Point-Statistics-and-Xray-Scattering.html) post as well as [this](http://phelpsforpresident.github.io/MIC-XRD-Polymer/2014/10/15/Autocorrelation-from-X-ray-Scattering-Data.html) post), we were unclear as to how to interpret the physical meaning of these real space plots. Late in the semester, we were able to meet with [Dr. Garmestani](http://www.mse.gatech.edu/faculty/garmestani) from the Materials Science and Engineering department at Georgia Tech. In previous [research](http://dx.doi.org/doi:10.1016/j.actamat.2011.01.013) his group was able to recover pair correlation function for a 2-phase material from SAXS data. From this information, we were able to [derive](http://nbviewer.ipython.org/github/phelpsforpresident/MIC-XRD-Polymer/blob/gh-pages/notebooks/Relationship_between_XRD_and_Autocorrelation.ipynb) the relationship to get the 2-point statistics from the SAXS images. $$ f^{11}( \pmb x) = \frac{1}{V \eta_o^2} \int I ( \pmb k) e^{-i\pmb k\pmb x} d\pmb k + V_1^2 $$ Unfortunately because we found this relationship too late in the semester to use the 2-point statistics for the linkage, but we are interested in using the 2-point statistics to do [microstructure reconstruction](dx.doi.org/doi:10.1016/j.actamat.2007.10.044) from the SAXS images. # Collaboration # Work Assignments # Trials - Success and Failures # Conclusion
